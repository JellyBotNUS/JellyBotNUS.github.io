Subsystem Overview

The Jellybot consists of four main subsystems working harmoniously to achieve efficient, biomimetic underwater motion and data collection. Each subsystem focuses on a key aspect of performance — from structure and propulsion to control and electronics.

1. Background

Importance of Coral Reefs:
Coral reefs are among the most species-rich ecosystems on Earth. They provide food, coastal protection, and habitat for marine life, yet are under severe threat from climate change, pollution, and human interference.

2. Literature Review

2.1 Introduction

Defining a value proposition is an essential step in identifying how technology can address critical environmental challenges. Existing underwater robotics platforms demonstrate various methods of propulsion and data collection, but few balance environmental sensitivity with efficiency.

2.2 Case Study

Research on biomimetic propulsion mechanisms has led to innovative underwater robots inspired by jellyfish motion. Such designs offer low-noise and energy-efficient movement — ideal for marine research without disturbing ecosystems.

3. Problem Clarification

Underwater ecosystems such as coral reefs face significant monitoring challenges due to the cost and complexity of traditional AUVs. JellyBot seeks to address this by offering a low-cost, environmentally friendly robotic solution capable of agile, non-intrusive underwater navigation.

4.3 Value Proposition Statement

Our jellyfish-inspired underwater robot, JellyBot, empowers marine researchers with a sustainable and affordable platform for long-duration ocean monitoring — minimizing environmental disruption while maximizing data insight.

5. User Journey

The JellyBot user journey emphasizes simplicity — from deployment to data collection. Operators can easily release the robot, collect sensor data autonomously, and retrieve insights through wireless transfer upon retrieval.

Figure 2: Simplified JellyBot user journey.

6. Features & Specifications

JellyBot incorporates lightweight mechanical components, low-power electronics, and a flexible propulsion system. Its modular design allows customization for different marine research purposes.
