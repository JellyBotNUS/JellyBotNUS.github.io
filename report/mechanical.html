1. Overview of the Mechanical System

The mechanical subsystem forms the structural backbone of Jellybot, ensuring durability, balance, and hydrodynamic efficiency. Designed with precision, it houses critical components such as the propulsion system, buoyancy chamber, and sensor mounts, all while maintaining waterproof integrity for extended underwater missions.

2. Structural Design and Materials

The Jellybot frame is constructed primarily from lightweight PVC and reinforced acrylic to provide both rigidity and corrosion resistance. The shell is pressure-sealed using silicone O-rings to prevent water ingress while allowing easy disassembly for maintenance and testing.

5. Summary and Future Improvements

The current mechanical design provides a strong foundation for Jellybot’s underwater operations. Future iterations may focus on lightweight composite materials, 3D-printed modular frames, and improved sealing mechanisms to further enhance performance and reliability.
