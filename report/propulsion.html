1. Overview of the Propulsion System

The propulsion subsystem enables Jellybot’s movement through a biomimetic design inspired by jellyfish locomotion. Using servo-driven actuation and flexible membranes, it generates rhythmic pulses that propel the robot smoothly through water. The design prioritizes energy efficiency, stability, and low noise for effective navigation in delicate marine environments.

4. Summary and Future Improvements

The propulsion subsystem successfully achieves biomimetic movement inspired by natural jellyfish dynamics, offering smooth and efficient underwater navigation. Its design prioritizes low energy consumption and mechanical simplicity. Future developments may explore adaptive propulsion materials, advanced servo control algorithms, and feedback-based motion tuning to enhance maneuverability and propulsion efficiency in complex underwater environments.
